Following a $110 million  German club Bayern Munich, plus $21 million in add-ons, the 30-year-old will wear a different jersey for the first time in years, ditching his Tottenham Hotspur kit for Bayern's red shirts.

Aside from wearing a new shirt, the Premier League's second-all-time topscorer will wear a new brand of boots, perhaps for the rest of his playing career.

Multiple reports say Kane  to a lifetime boot deal with American shoe company Skechers that is set to make its foray into the sport. Figures and details of the deal were not disclosed.

"New boots. New club. New season. Let's go," Kane  social media platform X, formerly known as Twitter, while showing off his new Skechers boots.

Kane is the company's first endorser signing for its football division, and he will be the face of the brand's first football boot, the SKX_01.

The shoe Kane previewed featured a primarily blue color fading lightly up to the toe. It has the signature Skechers "S" logo on the side, a usual sight in most of the company's shoes, this time in neon green. The boot is set to launch in the United Kingdom and Europe this coming September.

Commenting on the new partnership, the former Tottenham Hotspur said that it is a time of transition in his career, and the England striker is thrilled to announce his signing with Skechers at the same time as making a big move in his football career by joining Bayern Munich.

The 2020-21 Premier League Playmaker of the Season also said that ever since meeting Skechers, the design team, and seeing the boots for the first time, he felt confident that Skechers was onto something special. 

"I've been wearing the boots for weeks, scoring in them in preseason. They're unlike anything I've experienced before," the three-time Premier League Golden Boot winner said.
